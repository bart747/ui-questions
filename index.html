Questions to Ask Yourself when Designing UIs

rearrange by tagname:
Do I know where I am? Is it settings or admin page? Which element tells me that? Was it easy to spot?
Will that term sound familiar to users? Is there any ambiguity?
Is it possible to undo/redo changes? How to go a step back? Is it safe to make mistakes?
Do I create big blocks of data? How to divide them into easy to grasp chunks?
Is this design pattern familiar to users? Is it clear to people with habits developed at Google/Amazon/eBay UIs.
Am I consistent with language and visual patterns? It can be confusing when two things perform similar actions but look very different.
Will that new option make it more error prone? Maybe I should keep it simple by avoiding extra functionality?
Have I assumed that people will use it paying full attention?
Are all those elements really essential? Avoid clutter and ornaments to keep UI easy to scan.
Want to make it cool and inviting? Do I also think about about scannability and data-ink ratio?
Is all needed data visible and handy? Users shouldn't be forced to remember it or go to a different page to get it.
Will that advanced user's thing slow down novices? Can I organize things in a way both groups will like?
Are error messages helpful? Are they precisely pointing out mistakes and suggest solutions?
